## ⚡ Quick Decision Tree

**Answer these 3 questions to find your starting level:**

### Question 1: How much Linux experience do you have?
- ☐ Never used Linux → **Level 1**
- ☐ Played with it a few times → **Level 1**
- ☐ Comfortable with basics → **Level 2**
- ☐ Can administrate servers → **Level 2-3**

### Question 2: Have you worked in production?
- ☐ No production experience → **Start 1 level lower**
- ☐ Some production experience → **Keep your level**
- ☐ Years of production experience → **Move 1 level higher**

### Question 3: Time available to study?
- ☐ 40 hours/week (full-time) → **4-6 weeks per level**
- ☐ 15 hours/week (part-time) → **2-3 months per level**
- ☐ 5 hours/week (hobby) → **6-12 months per level**

## 💡 Pro Tips

### Tip 1: Practice on Real/Virtual Linux
Don't just read—you MUST practice on actual Linux systems:
- ✅ Use Virtual Box or VMware for VMs
- ✅ Or use cloud free tier (AWS EC2, Google Cloud)
- ✅ Or use WSL2 on Windows

### Tip 2: Type, Don't Copy
- ❌ Don't copy-paste commands
- ✅ Type commands manually
- Why? Builds muscle memory, faster learning

### Tip 3: Break Things (Safely)
- Intentionally break your VM
- Fix it yourself
- This is how you learn fastest

### Tip 4: Keep a Journal
- Write down what you learn
- Document commands you use
- Review your notes weekly

### Tip 5: Do All Hands-On Tasks
- Don't skip exercises
- They're designed to reinforce learning
- Practice > Theory

## 📞 Need Help?

**Q: I'm stuck on a task**  
A: Reread the scenario, Google the error, ask in DevOps communities

**Q: Which level should I start?**  
A: Use the decision tree above. When in doubt, start lower level.

**Q: Can I do multiple levels at once?**  
A: Not recommended. Focus on one level at a time.

**Q: How do I know if I'm ready to move to next level?**  
A: Complete all items in the level's checklist.

**Q: What if I skip a level?**  
A: You'll struggle. Each level builds on the previous. Go back.

**Q: How often should I study?**  
A: Consistency > duration. Better to study 1 hour daily than 8 hours once/week.
